Background

The Equity in Athletics Disclosure Act (EADA) is a federal law passed in 1994 that was designed to make prospective students and prospective student-athletes aware of a schools commitment to providing equitable athletic opportunities for its students. Because of the EADA, all colleges that receive Title IV funding are required to make their athletics data publicly available. There are many variables that each college must report our MWrite data sets contain some of these variables. During this supplement, you will complete two different statistical analyses, outlined below.

Be sure to load in any required packages first!

Analysis 1: Independent Scenario

Data (eada)

The first data set, eada, contains a random sample of 169 schools (that compete in Division 1 athletics). For these schools, the following information has been recorded for the 2021-2022 academic year:

  • school_type: whether the school is private or public

  • recruiting_total: the amount of money spent on recruiting male and female student athletes (in thousands of dollars)

The second data set, public, includes only the schools listed as Public from the previous eada data set. Additionally, we have broken down the original variables to include the funding split up by men and women (as well as variables for the differences between them):

  • expenses_women: the amount of money spent on womens athletic programs (in millions of dollars)

  • expenses_diff: the difference between the amount of money spent on mens and womens athletic programs (in millions of dollars)

Note: the three difference variables are calculated as men minus women Note: be sure to pay close attention to the units!

6. First, choose a variable for your analysis. Then, explain why this analysis is a paired (or dependent) scenario. What is the linking factor?

Options: Copy one of the options below and paste it into Answer 1

  • difference in the amount of money spent on student aid for male and female athletes
  • difference in the amount of money spent on recruiting male and female student athletes
  • difference in the amount of money spent on mens and womens athletic programs

Answer 1: We wish to assess if, on average, there is a difference in the amount of money spent on recruiting men and women for all public schools.

Answer 2: This analysis is a paired (or dependent) scenario because it involves comparing two related measurements on the same set of schools. In this case, the linking factor is the same set of public schools, and you are interested in the difference in funding between mens and womens recruiting programs for each of these schools. Each pair consists of two related measurements: the funding for mens programs and the funding for womens programs within the same school. This type of analysis is often used to determine whether there is a significant difference within pairs, which is why its considered a paired scenario.
